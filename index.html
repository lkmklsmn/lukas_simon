about

Decoding cancer with data


I'm an Assistant Professor and Group Leader of Computational Biology at the Therapeutic Innovation Center, Baylor College of Medicine. My work sits at the intersection of machine learning and cancer medicine—using AI and genomics to identify drug targets, understand disease mechanisms, and accelerate the path from discovery to therapy.

Before Baylor, I trained as a postdoctoral fellow with Fabian Theis at the Helmholtz Research Center in Munich, where I helped develop some of the first deep learning approaches for single-cell RNA sequencing. I also teach a graduate-level deep learning course at the University of St. Thomas.

research

What I work on

01
RNA Splicing & Cancer Therapeutics
Uncovering how MYC-driven tumors are selectively vulnerable to spliceosome-targeted therapies. This work contributed to a clinical-stage drug discovery program and a biotech spinoff.
02
Deep Learning for Single-Cell Genomics
Designing novel architectures—denoising autoencoders, triplet neural networks—to extract biological signal from the high-dimensional noise of single-cell sequencing data.
03
Biomarker Discovery in Oncology
Integrating patient genomic and functional screening data to find molecular markers that predict cancer cell vulnerabilities and guide drug development decisions.
04
Translational Multi-omic Analysis
Collaborating with wet-lab and clinical teams to translate large-scale molecular data—from lung cell atlases to platelet biology—into biological insight and therapeutic hypotheses.
